Girls Hoops (Age 13) — 3 Day Plan

Safe • Simple • Basketball-specific Repeat 4-week cycle

How it works: Pick **Day A, Day B, or Day C** each practice day. In a month, you’ll do each one several times. Keep movements smooth, never rushed. If anything hurts (sharp), stop.

Warm-Up (8–10 min)

  • Easy move (3–4 min): fast walk, light jog, or jump rope.
  • Body wake-up (2 rounds):
    • 10 arm circles forward + 10 back (stand tall).
    • 10 bodyweight squats (feet shoulder-width, heels down).
    • 10 glute bridges (lie on back, push hips up, squeeze butt).
    • 10 step-ups each leg (low box/step, quiet feet).
  • Ball feel (60–90s): gentle pound dribbles R/L, side-to-side.
Goal: feel warm, joints happy, breathing calm. You should be ready, not tired.

Day A — Strong Legs & Straight-Ahead Speed

~35–45 min
  • Goblet Squat — 3 sets × 6–8 reps
    Hold 1 dumbbell close to your chest. Feet under shoulders. Sit down like a chair, keep heels down and chest up. Stand tall. (No weight? Hug a backpack.)
  • Hip Hinge (RDL) — 3×8
    Hold 2 dumbbells or 1. Soft knees. Push hips back like you’re closing a car door with your butt. Keep back flat. Stand up by squeezing your butt.
  • Half-Kneeling DB Press — 3×8 each side
    One knee down, other foot forward. Squeeze your butt and tummy. Press the dumbbell straight up, lower slow. Switch sides.
  • Quick Shuttle (straight) — 4–6 runs × 10–15 m
    Sprint to the line, touch it with your hand, run back. Stand up tall between reps. Rest until breathing feels ready.
  • Small Jumps (Pogos) — 3×6–8 jumps
    Hands on hips, tiny quick jumps off the balls of your feet. Land soft. Stop if it gets loud or sloppy.
  • Core — Dead Bug — 3×6 each side
    On your back, arms up, knees up. Slowly reach opposite arm/leg away, keep lower back on floor. Switch sides.
  • Conditioning — Run/Walk — 6–8 rounds
    Run 20 sec (easy-fast), walk 40 sec. Keep all runs the same speed, not a sprint.

Day B — Side-to-Side & Upper Back

~35–45 min
  • Split Squat — 3×6 each leg
    Long step like a lunge. Front foot flat. Bend both knees, drop straight down, then up. Torso tall. Add dumbbells if easy.
  • 1-Arm DB Row — 3×8 each side
    One hand on bench or knee. Back flat. Pull weight to ribs. Lower slow. Switch sides.
  • Hip Bridge — 3×12
    On back, feet flat. Push hips up, squeeze butt 1 second, lower slow. Knees don’t fall out.
  • Lateral Shuffle — 3–5 sets
    Two cones ~5–6 m apart. Shuffle right to left and back (stay low, chest up, quiet feet). 10–15 sec each set.
  • Line Hops (Side) — 3×10 hops
    Jump side-to-side over a line. Small, quick, soft landings.
  • Core — Side Plank — 3×20–30 sec each side
    Elbow under shoulder, body straight like a board. Squeeze tummy and butt.
  • Easy Bike/Row/Walk — 8–10 min
    Smooth pace, you can talk the whole time.

Day C — Stops/Starts & Core Control

~35–45 min
  • Box Step-Ups — 3×8 each leg
    Knee-high or lower box. Step up with whole foot on box, stand tall, step down soft. Switch legs. Add dumbbells if easy.
  • DB RDL → High Pull (light) — 3×6
    Do a small hip hinge, then stand fast and pull weights to chest height (elbows high), lower slow. Light weights only; smooth.
  • Farmer Carry — 3×30–40 m
    Hold a dumbbell in each hand. Walk tall, shoulders down/back, quiet steps. No leaning.
  • 5-10-5 Shuttle — 3–5 sets
    Start in middle. Sprint 5 m → back 10 m → back 5 m. Touch each line. Rest until ready.
  • Snap Jumps — 3×3
    Quarter squat, jump small, land soft in the same spot. Stick the landing for 1 second.
  • Core — Pallof Press — 3×10 each side
    Band or cable at chest height. Stand sideways to it. Press arms straight out, don’t let your body twist, bring back in.
  • Fartlek Run — 10–12 min
    Mostly easy jog. Every minute, run a little faster for 10–15 sec, then back to easy.

Cool Down (5–7 min)

  • Slow walk and deep breaths (2–3 min).
  • Stretch: calf, hamstring, quad, hip flexor (about 30–40 sec each side).
  • Shoulders: doorway stretch (hands on door frame, gentle lean, 20–30 sec).
You should feel calmer and ready to go on with your day.

How to Make It a Little Harder Each Week

  • Week 1: Learn the moves. Use light weight. 2–3 sets.
  • Week 2: Add 1 set on the first two strength moves.
  • Week 3: Add 2 reps to each set or add a tiny weight (2.5–5 lb).
  • Week 4: Easier week. Do one fewer set. Keep the form clean.
Safety first: Movements should feel smooth and quiet. Pain = stop. Light and perfect beats heavy and sloppy.
